The Science

What are The brain waves — and why do they matter?

Your brain operates in different electrical frequency states throughout the day. Each state is linked to a distinct mental experience:

  • ●
    Delta (0.5–4 Hz)Deep dreamless sleep and physical recovery
  • ★
    The (4–8 Hz) — the “genius state”Deep relaxation, creative flow, memory consolidation and heightened learning. Associated with experienced meditators, peak-performance athletes and breakthrough creative thinking.
  • ●
    Alpha (8–13 Hz)Calm, relaxed alertness — the state just before sleep or after waking
  • ●
    Beta (13–32 Hz)Active thinking and alert focus — where most people spend their entire waking day
  • ●
    Gamma (32+ Hz)Peak cognitive processing and high-level information binding

The problem: most adults almost never enter the The state while awake. Modern life locks most brains into Beta — reactive, stressed, scattered. The Genius Wave is designed to change that.

What peer-reviewed research shows

Studies on brainwave entrainment — using rhythmic audio stimuli to influence brain frequency — have shown measurable effects on cognitive performance, stress reduction, and sustained focus when subjects are guided toward the Theta range. The underlying mechanism is well-documented in neuroscience literature, though individual results vary.
